1. Understanding Blockchain: Beyond Cryptocurrency

Blockchain, at its core, is a decentralized ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers in a way that ensures transparency, security, and immutability. Unlike traditional centralized systems, where data is stored in a single location, blockchain distributes data across multiple nodes, making it virtually tamper-proof. Each block in the blockchain contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, creating a chain of blocks that are linked together, hence the name "blockchain."

While blockchain's initial applications were predominantly in the realm of cryptocurrency, its potential extends far beyond digital currencies. Its decentralized nature and cryptographic security features make it applicable to a wide range of use cases, including supply chain management, digital identity verification, voting systems, and now, app development.
